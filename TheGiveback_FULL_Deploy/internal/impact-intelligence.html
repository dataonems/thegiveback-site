Why This Page Exists

Every giving platform has
the same credibility gap.
We're closing it.

When a foundation reports their impact, they write the report. When a charity tells you 90% of donations go to programs, they calculated that number. When a nonprofit says 87 youth were served, they counted the attendance sheets themselves.

None of that is dishonest. But it's not the same as independent measurement. And donors, merchants, and community members have started noticing the difference.

"The question isn't whether money moved. It's whether the money did something. Those are completely different questions and only one of them has ever been answered."

We built the infrastructure to answer the second question — not with surveys and self-reporting, but with objective engagement measurement that no foundation can fabricate and no donor has to simply trust.

How it works today

Everywhere else

Foundation runs a program. Counts the people in the room. Asks them on the way out if it was helpful. Writes up the results. Sends to funders. Funders receive the report. Report is exactly as positive as the foundation needs it to be.

Nobody can verify whether the program actually held attention, engaged participants, or changed anything. The best-run and worst-run programs produce identically formatted impact reports.

📊 How it works here

TheGiveback Verified

Foundation runs a program instrumented by AI-powered measurement tools. Objective engagement data extracted from the session. A Program Effectiveness Score generated on a standardized scale. The foundation gets to see and annotate before publishing — but they don't get to change the score.

The data tells the truth regardless of whether it makes the program look good. That honesty is what makes it trustworthy — and what makes the merchants, consumers, and donors who fund it confident their support is working.
