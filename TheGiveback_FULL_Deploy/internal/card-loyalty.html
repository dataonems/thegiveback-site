The Insight

Other cards reward you.
This one rewards
your neighborhood.

Every loyalty card in your wallet promises you something — miles, cashback, hotel nights. They're funded by interchange revenue the card issuer earns from your purchases. The money flows from merchants through networks through banks and into your points account.

The Giveback Card uses the same economics — but routes the giving to two destinations instead of one. Your community foundation gets funded from the merchant's acquiring margin. Your points account gets funded from the card's issuing interchange. Both happen from the same transaction. Neither one requires anything extra from you.

"When you buy your coffee at Rosa's with the Giveback Card, that transaction funds the food pantry twice — once from the restaurant's processing margin, once from your card's interchange. You get points. The community gets funded. Rosa's gets a customer who's invested in her success."

How you redeem

Three paths. Each one honest. The first one is the mission.

🏛️
Donate to a foundation Most meaningful
Convert your points directly to giving for any vetted foundation in the TheGiveback network. 100 points = $1 to your chosen cause. It appears as a separate, named entry on the public ledger: "Given by Sarah J's points redemption." Your giving, your name, your record.
🏪
Discount at certified merchants Keeps it local
Redeem points for a discount at any certified merchant storefront — in-store or online. Every point you redeem at a certified merchant is a dollar that stays inside the local commerce ecosystem, funding more giving on the next transaction.
💳
Statement credit Simple fallback
100 points = $1 off your card balance. Clean, simple, no games. Available always, but we'd love it if you gave it to someone first.

Why We Built It This Way

Bilt Rewards lost Wells Fargo $10M/month.
Here's why that won't happen here.

Bilt rewarded rent payments — transactions that generate near-zero interchange revenue — while funding rich travel rewards from that thin margin. The economics never worked. Wells Fargo lost tens of millions and exited the partnership.

We studied that failure before designing a single feature. The Giveback Card is built on the opposite structure: merchant-present card interchange on certified merchant transactions — the highest interchange category. The economics support the loyalty program.

What Bilt got wrong
  • Rewarded rent payments (near-zero interchange)
  • Rich travel rewards funded from thin margin
  • Complex dual currency on launch day
  • Bank partner absorbed unsustainable losses
  • Redemption catalog designed to impress, not sustain
What we built instead
  • Points earned on merchant-present transactions (high interchange)
  • Simple 3-path redemption — donate, discount, credit
  • No airline partners, no hotel tiers, no complexity
  • Durbin-exempt sponsor bank preserves interchange economics
  • The mission IS the value proposition — not travel perks

Quick Answers

The questions you're already asking.

? Is this a credit or debit card?
Debit card, at launch. Issued through a BaaS partner (Marqeta or Galileo) on top of a Durbin-exempt sponsor bank. This keeps the interchange economics in the range needed to fund the loyalty program. A credit product may follow once the debit program proves the unit economics.
? What if there are no certified merchants near me?
Your card still earns 1 point per dollar on any purchase, anywhere. And you can help change that — every certified merchant you refer earns you 500 bonus points. The more people who carry the card, the more merchants have a reason to join the network.
? Can I choose which foundation my giving goes to?
Yes. When you redeem points for a foundation donation, you choose from any vetted foundation in the network. Your card's automatic giving (from the interchange) defaults to the matched cause of the merchant where you spent. You always control your points redemption.
? When can I start earning points before the card launches?
Phase 3 of the platform introduces card-linking via Pledge API — connecting your existing card to certified merchant transactions so you start earning Giveback Points immediately. No branded card needed yet. The points carry over when the card launches.
? Why no airline or hotel transfer partners?
Because that's not what this is. The Giveback Card's value proposition is giving your points meaning in your community — not accumulating miles for a trip to Bali. Adding airline partners would make the program look like every other premium card and dilute the one thing that makes it different.
? Do my points expire?
Never. Points don't expire. They don't get devalued without notice. They don't require a minimum balance to redeem. This is a deliberate design decision — points that expire punish loyal customers. We're not building a gotcha product.
